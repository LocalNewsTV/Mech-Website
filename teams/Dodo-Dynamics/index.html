Dodo Dynamics

Introduction

DoDo Dynamics, a team of five students from three different countries, is on a mission to reinvent recreational boating. Their goal for this project revolves around enabling a canoe to achieve sustained flight approximately 2 ft above the water's surface, with a maximum load capacity of 600 lbs. The team aims to accomplish this by retrofitting hydrofoils and implementing an electric powertrain into an existing canoe. The objective is to mitigate air and noise pollution typically associated with boating on lakes, while simultaneously injecting a thrilling and exhilarating experience.

Project

In traditional water sports, there exists a dichotomy between two distinct experiences. On one end, lake boating and jet skiing are intensive and can be disruptive to the environment. On the other end, canoeing provides a serene and tranquil experience, albeit at a slower pace. Recognizing the potential in merging the advantages of both worlds, DoDo Dynamics is presently engaged in the design and construction of an Electric Hydrofoil Canoe. The primary objective of this endeavor is to create a vessel that embodies the best of both realms: speed and excitement, coupled with a quiet and affordable nature. The core aim is to achieve a state of stable and level flight, accomplished by lifting the canoe hull above the water's surface at a takeoff speed of four meters per second. To accomplish this, the canoe will be equipped with twin electric motors and marine batteries, which will serve as the power source for the innovative hydrofoil system.
